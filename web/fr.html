Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum investment to start a business in UAE?

The minimum investment to start a business in the UAE varies significantly based on several factors.

Firstly, the location of your business matters. Free zones often have lower entry barriers, with some allowing startups to begin with as little as AED 10,000. Mainland businesses typically require a higher investment, often starting from AED 300,000.

 

Secondly, the type of business you establish influences the cost. Service-based businesses generally have lower startup costs compared to trading or manufacturing companies.

 

Lastly, additional expenses like office space, permits, and visas will contribute to the overall investment.

How much is a Dubai business visa?

The cost of a Dubai business visa varies depending on several factors:

  • Type of visa: there are different types of business visas, each with its own fee structure.
  • Duration of stay: the visa validity affects the cost.
  • Additional fees: medical tests, Emirates ID, and processing fees can add to the overall expense.

 

Generally, you can expect to pay between AED 3,500 to AED 7,500 for a business visa. However, it's essential to consider the additional costs mentioned above. For a more accurate estimate, we highly recommend consulting with a business setup specialist in Dubai.

How is an LLC formed in the UAE?

  1. Choose a Business Activity: determine the specific activities your LLC will undertake. Then, choose a location and reserve a business name.
  2. Appoint a Local Sponsor: a UAE national must hold a 51% share, though profit-sharing arrangements can vary.
  3. Draft Memorandum of Association (MoA): outline company structure, shareholder details, and business activities.
  4. Obtain Necessary Approvals: secure permits and licenses from relevant authorities and submit necessary documents to the DED, before finally opening a corporate bank account.

Note: While this is a general overview, specific requirements and procedures may vary depending on the emirate and type of business. It's advisable to seek guidance from a local business setup consultant like Vision Prime for accurate and up-to-date information.

How long is the business visa valid for in Dubai?

The validity of a Dubai business visa depends on the type of visa:

  • Short-term business visas are typically valid for 30 to 90 days. These are suitable for business meetings, conferences, or initial market research.
  • Long-term business visas can be valid for up to 3 years and are often associated with setting up a business or working in the UAE.

 

It's important to note that visa regulations can change, so it's always recommended to check with the UAE immigration authorities or a business setup consultant for the most current information.

Can you own a 100% business in Dubai?

Yes, you can now own 100% of a business in Dubai.

This is a significant change from previous regulations. However, it's important to note that this applies to specific sectors and activities. It's advisable to check the eligibility of your business activity before proceeding.

 

While free zones have traditionally offered 100% foreign ownership, the recent amendments have extended this possibility to mainland businesses as well, subject to certain conditions.

Which business is easy to start in Dubai?

Dubai is a great place to start a business, with plenty of opportunities for entrepreneurs. Here are a few business sectors that are relatively easy to dive into:

  • E-commerce: with Dubai’s booming online market, setting up an e-commerce store can be a smart move. The barriers to entry are low, and you can reach a global audience with ease. Plus, it’s a flexible business model that can grow with you.
  • Freelancing: thanks to Dubai’s freelance visa, it’s easier than ever for professionals to go solo. Digital marketing, IT, and business consulting are just a few of the in-demand services you can offer.
  • Food and Beverage: Dubai’s lively food scene offers plenty of room for cafes, food trucks, and restaurants. The city’s diverse population means you’ll have a steady stream of customers.

 

Many of these businesses benefit from Dubai’s free zones, which make the setup process smoother and offer perks like tax benefits and full foreign ownership.

Is Dubai startup-friendly?

Dubai is definitely a hotspot for startups.

The city has built a thriving ecosystem that supports entrepreneurship with some key advantages:

  • Business-Friendly Regulations: easy procedures for setting up and running a company.
  • Tax Incentives: great tax benefits that make starting up even more attractive.

Is Dubai tax-free for business owners?

Dubai is well-known for its tax-friendly environment, which is a major draw for entrepreneurs and investors alike.

Currently, the UAE offers zero personal income tax and no corporate tax on most businesses. However, from June 2023, a corporate tax of 9% will be introduced for businesses exceeding a profit threshold of AED 375,000.

 

Free zones in Dubai, however, will continue to offer tax incentives, including the possibility of 100% foreign ownership, exemption from import/export duties, and full repatriation of profits.

Therefore, Dubai remains a favorable destination for businesses seeking to minimize tax liabilities.

How to make money in Dubai as a foreigner?

Dubai offers a multitude of opportunities for foreigners to earn money. Here are some popular avenues:

  • Start a Business: Dubai's free zones offer favorable conditions for starting your own business with 100% foreign ownership, making it easier to venture into industries such as trading, IT, and consulting.
  • Invest in Real Estate: Dubai's property market is one of the most lucrative in the world, offering high rental yields and capital appreciation.
  • Freelancing: The UAE has introduced a freelance visa, allowing professionals in fields like media, IT, and education to offer their services legally in Dubai.
  • Teaching: If you have expertise in a specific subject or skill, you can teach it either in institutions or as a private tutor.

 

Additionally, Dubai’s strategic location as a global hub makes it a prime spot for those looking to import/export goods or offer international business services.

Dubai has become a magnet for entrepreneurs for several compelling reasons:

  • Business-Friendly Environment: Dubai’s government has created an environment that encourages business growth with easy company registration processes, minimal bureaucracy, and supportive regulations.

These factors make Dubai an attractive destination for entrepreneurs looking to start or expand their businesses globally.

What is the cheapest way to set up a business in Dubai?

Setting up a business in a free zone is usually the most budget-friendly option in Dubai. Free zones offer various packages with different perks and cost structures.

Here’s a quick breakdown:

  • Free Zones: these zones provide flexible packages that often include office space, visa options, and other benefits. Some popular and cost-effective free zones are:
    • Creative Zone
    • Dubai Media City
    • Sharjah Media City
  • Mainland: setting up a business on the mainland can be pricier because you’ll need a local sponsor and may have higher office space costs. However, it does offer more flexibility in terms of business activities.
  • E-commerce: this can be a low-cost option, especially if you choose a dropshipping model, which means you won’t have to worry about managing inventory or warehousing.

 

Overall, free zones are a great way to keep costs down while still enjoying plenty of benefits.

Can I open a company in the UAE without a residence visa?

Yes, you can open a company in Dubai and conduct business without a residence visa.

In Dubai, foreign entrepreneurs can start a company without needing a residence visa, thanks to the city’s business-friendly environment. You can achieve this by setting up in one of Dubai’s free zones, which offer several perks:

  • 100% Foreign Ownership: you can fully own your business without needing a local sponsor.
  • Simplified Setup: getting your company up and running in a free zone is usually faster and easier than on the mainland.
  • Tax Benefits: many free zones provide corporate tax exemptions.

 

However, keep in mind:

  • Visa Requirements for Business Operations: while you don’t need a residence visa to establish your company, you might need one to visit Dubai for business or to hire employees.
  • Business Activity Restrictions: some business activities might be limited to specific free zones.

How much is a business permit in Dubai?

The cost of a business permit in Dubai can vary widely based on several factors:

  • Type of License: the activities your business will undertake determine the type of license you need, and each comes with different fees.
  • Location: setting up in a free zone is usually more affordable than on the mainland.
  • Business Structure: whether you’re a sole proprietor or forming a limited liability company, the legal structure affects the cost.
  • Additional Fees: don’t forget about extra costs like office space rental, visa fees, and professional services.

 

On average, you might pay between AED 8,000 to AED 30,000 for a basic business license. However, the exact amount can vary based on your specific situation.

While free zones have always allowed 100% foreign ownership, the changes now extend to mainland business setups, offering greater flexibility and opportunities for entrepreneurs.

It's essential to note that not all business activities are eligible for 100% foreign ownership. Certain sectors may still require a local sponsor.

Can I start a business in Dubai without an office?

Yes, You Can Start a Business in Dubai Without a Physical Office.

Dubai has embraced the digital age and offers flexible options for entrepreneurs. You can indeed start a business without a physical office space.

Here are the primary methods:

  • Virtual Office License: many free zones in Dubai offer virtual office licenses, allowing you to register a business without requiring a physical office. This option is ideal for service-based businesses.
  • Flexi Desk Permits: some free zones provide flexi desk permits, granting you access to shared workspace facilities without committing to a dedicated office.
  • Free Zone Licenses: certain free zones allow businesses to operate without a physical office, focusing on online activities.

Is home business allowed in Dubai?

The Dubai government sees the potential in small and medium enterprises (SMEs) and has introduced home business licenses to support entrepreneurs. This means you can legally run a business right from your home.

While there are some guidelines and regulations to follow, starting a home-based business in Dubai is a practical and achievable option for many.
